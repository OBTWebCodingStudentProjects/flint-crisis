In April 2014 Flint switched its water source to the Flint River. This was meant to be "cost saving" but proved to be detrimental for the city. While residents were explicitly not warned of the aging pipes and the potential health problems they were instead issued a boil water advisory. A General moters plant however, would later switch its water source after realizing that the water was corroding newly installed car parts.

The problem could have ended here when in January 2015, Detroit offered to recconect to Flint free of charge. However Flint officials declined the offer stating there was no issue. A woman would later notify the E.P.A after her son developed rashes from the water in their home. They would discover 104 parts per billion of lead in her home - 15 parts per billion is what is considered "safe". A second testing two months later would find 397 parts per billion and similar lead levels in more homes in Flint. Flint later connects back to Detroit as a water source.

The city of Flint takes it upon itself to declare a state of emergency. The Governer later declares a state of emergency as well in Flint, and President Obama also declares a state of emergency, granting up to $5 million in aid. The EPA also steps in to take over water testing after the Michigan Department of Environmental Quality is deemed incompetent and criticized for poor handling of the situation.
